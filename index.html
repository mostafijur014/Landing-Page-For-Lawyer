Common Questions

Understanding Your Position

How are assets divided in a divorce in the UK?

+
There is no fixed formula. The court considers a range of factors including financial contributions, future needs, and overall asset structure. In higher value cases, the details matter significantly. Each situation is assessed on its own merits, making early strategic advice especially important.

What happens to a business in a divorce?

+
A business can form part of the financial settlement. The extent of its involvement depends on ownership, valuation, and how it fits into the wider financial picture. In cases where business interests are significant, the approach taken from the outset is critical to protecting your position.

Can assets be protected before divorce?

+
In certain situations, steps can be taken early on that may influence how assets are treated. Timing and structure are critical, and decisions should be made carefully and with proper legal guidance. What you do — or fail to do — in the early stages can have lasting consequences.

When should I seek legal advice?

+
Often, the earlier you understand your position, the more options remain available. Waiting too long can limit flexibility and reduce the strategic choices available to you. Even if you are at an early or exploratory stage, understanding the landscape is valuable.
